Nexora Technologies, founded by CEO Zara Klingston, is headquartered in Velmont City, New Arcadia. The company specializes in quantum mesh networking and was established in 2019.

Zara Klingston previously served as CTO of Luminar Systems before founding Nexora. Under her leadership, Nexora acquired DataVault Inc. in 2021 for $2.3 billion.

Nexora's main competitor, CyberDyne Solutions, led by Marcus Reeves, is based in Neo Angeles. The two companies jointly developed the OpenMesh protocol in 2022.

Key Nexora executives include:
- CTO: Dr. Elena Vasquez (joined from Quantum Works)
- CFO: Robert Chen (formerly of FinanceCore)
- VP Engineering: Sarah Mitchell

In 2023, Nexora partnered with AeroLink Industries to develop next-generation satellite communication systems. The partnership, valued at $450 million, aims to deploy quantum-encrypted mesh networks across 47 orbital platforms.

Nexora's research division, headed by Dr. Yuki Tanaka, operates three facilities:
- Velmont Research Center (headquarters)
- Neo Tokyo Innovation Lab
- Crystal Bay Development Campus

The company's board of directors includes:
- Chairperson: Jennifer Wu (former CEO of TechVentures Capital)
- Independent Director: Michael Sterling
- Independent Director: Dr. Amara Okonkwo

Nexora Technologies went public in 2022 with an IPO valuation of $8.7 billion. The company's stock trades on the Neo Exchange under the symbol NXRA.
